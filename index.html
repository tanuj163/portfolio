Tanuj Gupta, Ph.D.

I'm

About

Mechanical engineer and computational materials researcher with a strong background in atomistic modeling, FEA, and multi-physics simulation. Experienced in leading simulation-driven research for nanoscale deformation and high-temperature sensing, with applications spanning materials design, sensor development, and machine learning.

Research Areas

  • Molecular dynamics (LAMMPS) simulations of gold nanostructures to uncover deformation mechanisms under extreme conditions
  • Finite element analysis and multi-physics modeling (ANSYS, COMSOL) for sensor design in industrial-scale environments
  • Development of machine learning workflows for data-driven classification of nanoscale deformation mechanisms
  • Expertise in simulation automation using Python and MATLAB; strong background in HPC and SLURM-based job scheduling
  • Hands-on experience with shock modeling, thermal diffusion, and acoustic wave simulation
  • Collaborative research with national labs, interdisciplinary university teams, and experimental validation partners
  • Presenter at top conferences (TMS, IMECE, EMI) and contributor to peer-reviewed journals and a PCT patent
  • Mentorship and teaching experience in molecular simulation, computational tools, and high-performance modeling

Research Interests

Machine Learning for Material Science

Data-driven classification and prediction of nanoscale material behavior using advanced ML algorithms and automation.

Nanoscale Materials

Study of mechanical behavior, deformation mechanisms, and surface effects in ultra-small metallic nanostructures.

Finite Element Analysis

Simulation of structural, thermal, and mechanical performance of components under real-world industrial conditions.

Multi-Physics Modeling

Integration of thermal, mechanical, acoustic, and electrical physics for accurate material response simulation.

Non-Destructive Testing

Techniques such as photoacoustic imaging and acoustic simulation for detecting material defects without damage.
